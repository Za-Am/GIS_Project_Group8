Landslide In Italy

In Italy, landslides are a serious threat to the population, from 1950 to 2015, 661 fatal landslides occurred causing 4105 fatalities, including 1910 estimated fatalities caused by the Vajont landslide (9 October 1963), the most destructive slope failure in Europe in historic time. In this period, the frequency of low magnitude events has remained unchanged, and the magnitude of the most destructive events has decreased. The reduced magnitude of the most destructive events is related largely to the increased availability of information, and to improved monitoring and warning systems.Please check this website.

  • Data

    Vector layers contain roads, rivers and are obtained from OpenStreet maps which is acollaborative project to create a free editable geographic database of the world. Fault and Digital Terrain Model layers are used from geoportal Regione Lombardia which is a system able to share the information provided by different sources and to make this information easily available to every user. And Normalized Difference Vegetation Index layer is taken from ESA Copernicus Sentinel-2 missions (year of interest 2020).
