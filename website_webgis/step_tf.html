Final Steps

The main focus in steps 3 and 4 is to compute the population in each class of susceptibility map




For steps 3 and 4 we reproject the population raster dataset for our region of interest. Reclassifying the susceptibility map(obtained in step 2)using four classes namely(Low, Moderate, High, and Very High) was our next phase. afterward, we resampled the reclassified susceptibility raster map with respect to the population raster dataset.

Conclusion

Computing the population counts in each susceptibility class and representing the results was the part of step 4. pie chart depicting the percentage of population per each susceptibility class was the by product of this step the result was highly complimentary. according to the pie chart, 98.2% of people inhabit places which the risk of landslides is low. On the other hand, almost 1.52% are at the risk of losing their life or property. so appropriate action is highly recommended.
